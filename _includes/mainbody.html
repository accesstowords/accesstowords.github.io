Written words are a problem for millions of adults. For a variety of reasons, English literacy is a barrier for 8 million adults.

Foreign Languages

English is not the first language of 4 million adults living in the UK. 2% of the adult population does not speak English, and many of those who do, find English literacy a challenge.

Deaf

Of 150,000 people who use British Sign Language, more than half rely on BSL as their first or only language. BSL is a different language to English, and there is no written form.

Blind and Vision Impaired

Spoken words might be the only way for 1 in 20 British adults with sight loss to recieve your messaging.

Illiteracy

As much as 20% of the UK population finds reading difficult, and prefer spoken to written words. 4% of adults are classified as having severe literacy difficulty.
