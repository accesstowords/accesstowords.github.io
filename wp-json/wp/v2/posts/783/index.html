There’s a whole range of technology helping D\/deaf people communicate, access information, and stay safe in their environment. Here’s a breakdown of some key areas:<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Communication Technologies<\/h2>\n\n\n\n
    \n
  • Sign Language Translation:<\/strong> AI is making strides in translating written words in sign language. These tools can bridge communication gaps.\u00a0<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Video Calling and Messaging with Sign Language Interpretation, such as Video Relay:<\/strong>  Video conferencing platforms with built-in sign language interpretation services can connect deaf individuals with others in real-time.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Text Relay Services: <\/strong>These services allow deaf and hearing people to communicate through typed messages relayed by a communication assistant.<\/li>\n\n\n\n
  • Easy-Read Translations:<\/strong> English can be difficult to read, particularly when it isn’t your first language. Many D\/deaf people prefer simplified English, and technologies now exist to transform complex wording into plain English.<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n\n\n\n
